About

How I approach design work

I came into UX/UI from digital marketing, so I pay close attention to user intent, message clarity, funnel friction, and what teams actually need to learn from a product flow. My focus is turning that evidence into decisions a team can test.

Evidence before claims

I separate what is proven, what is assumed, and what still needs validation.

Clear workflows first

I prefer focused product flows that are understandable before they are expanded into larger systems.

Process

How I work through problems

My process moves from evidence and problem framing to interface decisions and validation.

01

Research and listen

Collect evidence from users, competitors, and the current workflow before deciding what to design.

02

Define the problem

Turn broad pain points into a clear user need, product boundary, and testable design requirement.

03

Ideate and structure

Map the flow, separate inputs from outputs, and choose the smallest concept that can be validated.

04

Prototype and refine

Use high-fidelity screens and interaction checks to test whether the product model is understandable.

05

Test and validate

Check comprehension, document what is unclear, and only expand the product after the core loop works.
